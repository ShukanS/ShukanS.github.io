Sports Rules

Spring 2023

NO SWEARING/RUDE LANGUAGE

  • Including towards or with Teammates

NO PHYSICAL PLAY

  • any play or action that is deemed aggressive or that can cause injury will be Penalised

INVOLVING TEAMMATES

  • Passing to and involving all teammates

  • Everyone should play an equal amount of time bar injury/physical limitations

RESPECTING CALLS

  • Self-calls -- for some sports we do not have referees, and will rely on players calling violations on their own

  • no arguments over calls

  • all players should understand to play by the honour system, and honour other players' calls

  • any notion of others abusing this system should be brought up by the team captain only to sports core team (Raj, Krish, Maya, Shukan)

CYG VOLLEYBALL RULES

THE SERVE:

  1. Server must serve from behind the end line
  2. Ball may be served underhand or overhand.
  3. Served ball may graze the net and drop to the other side for a point.
  4. Each player will get 2 chances to successfully serve the ball.

SCORING:

  1. Rally scoring will be used.
  2. Game will be played to 21 points.
  3. Must win by 2 points.
  4. Time limit is 8 minutes. If neither team has reached 21 points, the team with the most points at the end of 8 mins wins the game,

ROTATION:

  1. Team will rotate each time they win the serve.
  2. Players shall rotate in a clockwise manner.
  3. There shall be 6 players on each side.

PLAYING THE GAME (VOLLEY)

  1. Maximum of three (3) hits per side.
  2. Player may not hit the ball twice in succession (a block is not considered a hit).
  3. Ball may be played off the net during a volley and on serve.
  4. A ball touching a boundary line is good.
  5. A legal hit is contact with the ball by a players' body above and including the waist which does not allow the ball to visibly come to a rest.
  6. A player must not block or attack a serve.

BASIC VIOLATIONS:

  1. Stepping on or over the line on a serve.
  2. Failure to serve the ball over the net successfully.
  3. Hitting the ball illegally (carrying, palming, throwing, etc).
  4. Excessive arguing that results in delay of gameplay will result in an automatic point for the other team and ejection from the game.

CYG BASKETBALL RULES

  • Games will be timed to 8 minutes.

  • There will be no stoppages in time except for in the final 2 minutes of a game.

SCORING

  • Baskets are worth 2 points, except for 3-point baskets, scored from behind the 3 point line.

  • Both of the player's feet must be behind the 3-point line in order for the basket to be worth 3 points, not in front of or on top of the line.

  • Baskets are scored by sinking the ball into the basketball hoop.

GAMEPLAY

  • Only 5 players from each team may be on the court at a time.

  • Each player (bar physical inability) must play an even amount of time.

  • Substitutions may be made during pauses in play.

  • Players must make an effort to pass the ball to all players on the floor.

  • Players may make forward progress by dribbling or passing the ball.

VIOLATIONS

  • A player may not move (take steps) without first dribbling the ball. This is a travelling violation and the ball will be returned to the opposing team.

  • Players may only dribble with one hand at a time.

  • Holding the ball with 2 hands while dribbling will be considered as "stopping your dribble".

  • Continuing to dribble the ball after "stopping your dribble" or dribbling with 2 hands is a double dribble violation and the ball will be returned to the opposing team.

  • The player's hand must be on top of the ball while dribbling. If they touch the bottom of the basketball while dribbling and continue to dribble this is called carrying the ball and the player will lose the ball to the other team.

  • If, at any time, both hands touch the ball or the player stops dribbling, the player must only move one foot. The foot that is stationary is called the pivot foot. You may not switch your pivot foot.

  • eg. If a player begins to pivot on their left foot, they may not then pivot on their right foot.

  • A player may only take 2 steps after stopping their dribble in motion, any more than that is a travelling violation.

  • The ball and player must remain in bounds. If the ball or a player in possession of the ball touches the floor or wall out of bounds, the ball is returned to the opposing team. The last player who touched the ball will be called for an out of bounds violation.

  • No player can touch the basketball while it is travelling downward towards the basket or if it is on the rim. This is called goaltending. (touching the ball on the rim is legal in some games). Goaltending will result in the basket being counted for the offensive team.

  • Basketball players cannot kick the ball or hit it with their fist.

FOULS

  • The main rule for the defensive player is not to foul. A foul is described as gaining an unfair advantage through physical contact.The defensive player may not touch the offensive player in a way that may harm or interfere with the offensive player

  • Although the foul rule is described above as a defensive rule, it applies exactly the same to all players on the court including offensive players.
