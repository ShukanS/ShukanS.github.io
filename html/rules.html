Sports Rules

Spring 2023

NO SWEARING/RUDE LANGUAGE

  • Including towards or with Teammates

NO PHYSICAL PLAY

  • any play or action that is deemed aggressive or that can cause injury will be Penalised

INVOLVING TEAMMATES

  • Passing to and involving all teammates

  • Everyone should play an equal amount of time bar injury/physical limitations

RESPECTING CALLS

  • Self-calls -- for some sports we do not have referees, and will rely on players calling violations on their own

  • no arguments over calls

  • all players should understand to play by the honour system, and honour other players' calls

  • any notion of others abusing this system should be brought up by the team captain only to sports core team (Raj, Krish, Maya, Shukan)

CYG VOLLEYBALL RULES

THE SERVE:

  1. Server must serve from behind the end line
  2. Ball may be served underhand or overhand.
  3. Served ball may graze the net and drop to the other side for a point.
  4. Each player will get 2 chances to successfully serve the ball.

SCORING:

  1. Rally scoring will be used.
  2. Game will be played to 21 points.
  3. Must win by 2 points.
  4. Time limit is 8 minutes. If neither team has reached 21 points, the team with the most points at the end of 8 mins wins the game,

ROTATION:

  1. Team will rotate each time they win the serve.
  2. Players shall rotate in a clockwise manner.
  3. There shall be 6 players on each side.

PLAYING THE GAME (VOLLEY)

  1. Maximum of three (3) hits per side.
  2. Player may not hit the ball twice in succession (a block is not considered a hit).
  3. Ball may be played off the net during a volley and on serve.
  4. A ball touching a boundary line is good.
  5. A legal hit is contact with the ball by a players' body above and including the waist which does not allow the ball to visibly come to a rest.
  6. A player must not block or attack a serve.

BASIC VIOLATIONS:

  1. Stepping on or over the line on a serve.
  2. Failure to serve the ball over the net successfully.
  3. Hitting the ball illegally (carrying, palming, throwing, etc).
  4. Excessive arguing that results in delay of gameplay will result in an automatic point for the other team and ejection from the game.

CYG BASKETBALL RULES

  • All rules will be applied at the discretion of the referee so as to not disrupt the general flow of play
  • Excessive arguing with a referee that results in delay of gameplay will result in an automatic technical foul and ejection from the game.
  • Games will be timed to 8 minutes.
  • There will be no stoppages in time.

SCORING

  • Baskets are worth 2 points, except for 3-point baskets, scored from behind the 3 point line.
    • Both of the player's feet must be behind the 3-point line in order for the basket to be worth 3 points, not in front of or on top of the line.
  • Baskets are scored by sinking the ball into the basketball hoop.

GAMEPLAY

  • Only 5 players from each team may be on the court at a time. Any more players on the court at a time will be a technical foul.
  • Each player (bar physical inability) must play an even amount of time.
  • Substitutions may be made during pauses in play.
  • Players must make an effort to pass the ball to all players on the floor.
  • Players may make forward progress by dribbling or passing the ball.

VIOLATIONS

  • A player may not move (take steps) without first dribbling the ball. This is a travelling violation and the ball will be returned to the opposing team.
  • Players may only dribble with one hand at a time.
  • Holding the ball with 2 hands while dribbling will be considered as "stopping your dribble".
  • Continuing to dribble the ball after "stopping your dribble" or dribbling with 2 hands is a double dribble violation and the ball will be returned to the opposing team.
  • The player's hand must be on top of the ball while dribbling. If they touch the bottom of the basketball while dribbling and continue to dribble this is called carrying the ball and the player will lose the ball to the other team.
  • If, at any time, both hands touch the ball or the player stops dribbling, the player must only move one foot. The foot that is stationary is called the pivot foot. You may not switch your pivot foot.
    • eg. If a player begins to pivot on their left foot, they may not then pivot on their right foot.
  • A player may only take 2 steps after stopping their dribble in motion, any more than that is a travelling violation.
  • The ball and player must remain in bounds. If the ball or a player in possession of the ball touches the floor or wall out of bounds, the ball is returned to the opposing team. The last player who touched the ball will be called for an out of bounds violation.
  • No player can touch the basketball while it is travelling downward towards the basket or if it is on the rim. This is called goaltending. (touching the ball on the rim is legal in some games). Goaltending will result in the basket being counted for the offensive team.
  • Basketball players cannot kick the ball or hit it with their fist.

FOULS

  • The main rule for the defensive player is not to foul. A foul is described as gaining an unfair advantage through physical contact.The defensive player may not touch the offensive player in a way that may harm or interfere with the offensive player
  • Although the foul rule is described above as a defensive rule, it applies exactly the same to all players on the court including offensive players.
  • Any intentional or flagrant fouls are banned and will result in a technical foul, 2 points being awarded to the opponent and a turnover of possession of the ball.
    • An intentional foul will be defined as contact against a player with or without the ball and that is deemed unnecessary or not within the bounds of play.
    • A flagrant foul will be defined as contact against a player with or without the ball and that is deemed unnecessary, aggressive and/or excessive.
  • Any player who accrues 2 technical fouls in a game shall be ejected from the game.
  • Accruing a technical foul in the last 2 minutes of play will award 2 points to the opponent.

CYG ULTIMATE RULES

START

  • The game starts with the ball in possession of one of the teams.

  • The opposing team must stand at least 10 feet away from the player that starts with possession. The thrower tries to throw the ball to a member of his/her own team.

  • To allow for faster play, after a goal is scored, the game can resume before all opponents are back on their side of the court (the team taking the throw-off, however, must still be on its own half).

TOUCHING

  • A player may stop, catch, throw, or strike the ball in any manner and in any direction, using hands, fists, arms, head, body, thighs or knees.

  • However, a player may not intentionally touch the ball with any part of the body below the knee. The ball is almost always played with the hands.

POSSESSION

  • A player in possession of the ball may stand stationary for only five seconds before passing.

  • A player can't pass to himself/herself.

  • If the ball hits the ground without a defensive player touching it, possession is awarded to the defensive team where the ball hits the ground.

  • If the ball hits the ground after deflecting off a defensive player, the ball is considered live and can be picked up by either team.

PASSING

  • Players typically advance the ball down the court by passing it between each other.

  • A player in possession of the ball may not take more than three steps, at which point they must pass the ball.

DEFENSE

  • Defensive players may not hold, hit, push or trip an offensive player.

  • Among the tactics a defensive player may use to gain control of the ball are using the flat part of the hand to knock the ball away from the offensive player.

  • It is forbidden to snatch the ball with one or both hands or violently strike or slap the ball from an opponent's hands.

  • Offensive players may set basketball-style screens in order to shield or free a teammate from a defensive player.

FOULS

  • If the player committing a foul is on the team not in possession of the ball, the referee may allow the play to continue until the offensive team scores a goal, loses possession or the action stops for other circumstances such as a free throw. This prevents an offensive advantage from being lost due to an undesired stoppage of play.

  • Depending on the severity of a foul, a free throw can be awarded.

SCORING

  • A team scores a point by successfully catching the ball within the opposing team's endzone.

  • The team that has more points at the end of the time limit wins.

  • If the scores are tied at the end of the time limit, the game will end in a tie.

OUT OF BOUNDS

  • If the entire ball passes across the sideline, the team that last touched the ball loses possession and the other team is awarded a throw-in.

SUBSTITUTIONS

  • There is no limit on substitutions, which can be made during the action, as in hockey, or during a time out.

  • During play, the athlete entering the game must wait until the player s/he is replacing has completely cleared the playing field.

CYG EXPRESS DODGEBALL RULES

  • Two Teams will be competing in each game of Express Dodge Ball.

  • Dodgeballs will be placed in the middle of the court.

  • The game starts with each team standing on their respective back line.

  • When the whistle blows, both teams rush to grab the balls and bring them back to their side.

  • Once a player has a ball, they can throw it at players on the other team.

  • If a player is hit by a ball, they are out and must leave the court.

  • If a player catches a ball thrown by the other team, the player who threw the ball is out.

  • Players cannot step over the boundary line or they will be out.

  • The game ends when all players on one team are eliminated, and the other team is declared the winner
